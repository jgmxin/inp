About DPE Gouges
A "gouge" is inside information — topics, scenarios, or questions a particular DPE is known to focus on during orals. Through our onsite DPE relationships and instructor network, we sometimes have access to this kind of insight for specific examiners. When a gouge is available for your examiner, we'll discuss it in your session. We don't guarantee gouges for every DPE, but when we have them, they're yours.

Session Format

What to Expect

Session Length
5–7
Hours — full day coverage
📹
Google Meet Online · No travel · Screen sharing
01
Pre-Session Logbook & Document Review
Before we start the oral topics, you'll share your logbook pages, endorsements, and knowledge test results. We verify everything is in order and flag any issues before the actual checkride.
02
ACS Topic-by-Topic Deep Dive
We work through every ACS task area methodically — regulations, weather, systems, performance, airspace, navigation, emergencies. Your instructor asks the question, you answer, then we discuss and build the concept until it sticks.
03
Scenario-Based Question Practice
Most examiners use scenarios rather than direct questions. We practice the "you're flying to X and Y happens — what do you do?" format so you're not caught off guard by open-ended questions on test day.
04
DPE Gouge Discussion
If we have specific insight on your examiner's focus areas, tendencies, or favorite topics, we'll cover those last so they're fresh. This part of the session can change your preparation significantly.
05
Checkride Binder Walkthrough
We go through your binder item by item — airworthiness documents, registration, POH, weight & balance, equipment list, maintenance records. You'll know exactly what to hand the examiner and in what order.
