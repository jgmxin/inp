The Process

How Time Building Works

We don't do ad-hoc rentals for time building. Aircraft are reserved and issued to you for the duration of your block — so you have guaranteed access every single day, no scheduling fights.

01
Choose Your Block
Select the aircraft and hour block that matches your goals — from 50 hours to 1,000+. Larger blocks get deeper discounts.
02
Aircraft Dispatched to You
Once insurance and qualifications are confirmed, the aircraft is yours for the entire block period. No sharing, no competing with other renters.
03
Fly Every Day
Minimum 6 hours per day / ~42 hours per week. Florida's 300+ VFR days per year mean almost nothing stops you from flying on schedule.
04
Solo or Shared
Fly solo as PIC, or split costs with another time builder — both options are structured. Shared time cuts your per-hour cost nearly in half.
05
Cross-Country Anywhere
Use Florida's ideal geography for meaningful XC routes — up and down the coasts, to the Bahamas, through Georgia and the Carolinas. Real PIC time, real experience.
06
Reach Your Target
Most pilots building 100–150 hours finish in about 4 weeks. At the 1,000+ hour block, you're on a direct runway to ATP minimums.

Before You Start

Requirements & Qualifications

Requirements differ slightly depending on whether you hold an FAA certificate or are an international pilot converting a foreign license.

🇺🇸 FAA Licensed Pilots
Valid FAA Private Pilot Certificate or higher
Current medical certificate (BasicMed accepted)
Current flight review (BFR) — we can provide one if needed
Renters insurance (~$93/month — required before dispatch)
Familiarization / check flight optional (not required)
Aircraft dispatched to you upon insurance confirmation
🌍 International Pilots
Valid foreign pilot license (ICAO CPL or PPL equivalent)
FAA Foreign Pilot Verification via IACRA (we assist with this)
FAA PPL required before aircraft dispatch — we can provide training
US Biannual Flight Review required upon receipt of FAA license
Renters insurance (~$93/month — required before dispatch)
FAA Third Class Medical or BasicMed
International Pilot Pathway
If you hold a foreign ICAO license and want to build FAA hours in the US, we assist with the entire conversion process. Florida is one of the best places in the world for international pilots to build hours — excellent weather, low airspace congestion outside major hubs, and direct routing to the Bahamas and Caribbean.
Step 1
Register on IACRA and begin FAA Foreign Pilot Verification process
Step 2
Obtain FAA PPL (we provide training if needed)
Step 3
Complete US Biannual Flight Review with our CFI
Step 4
Obtain renters insurance & aircraft is dispatched to you
Step 5
Fly your block — minimum 6 hrs/day across Florida & beyond
