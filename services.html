Beyond the Certificate

Other Services

Endorsements, proficiency checks, logbook services, and specialized training to expand your privileges, stay current, and fly with more confidence.

⚙
Complex Endorsement
Logbook Endorsement · FAR 61.31(e)

Required to act as PIC of a complex aircraft — one with retractable gear, flaps, and a controllable-pitch propeller. Train in the Piper Arrow or equivalent complex single.

Ground instruction covering systems and operating procedures
Flight training in a qualified complex aircraft
Logbook endorsement upon demonstrated proficiency
No minimum hours required — completion based
↑
High Performance Endorsement
Logbook Endorsement · FAR 61.31(f)

Required to act as PIC of any aircraft with an engine over 200 horsepower. A logical next step after your complex endorsement for pilots building toward commercial privileges.

Ground training on high-performance engine operations
Flight training in a qualifying HP aircraft
Covers MP, prop management, and fuel injection
Logbook endorsement upon demonstrated proficiency
✦
Tailwheel Endorsement
Logbook Endorsement · FAR 61.31(i)

Flying a conventional-gear ("tailwheel") aircraft requires a separate endorsement. Tailwheel training sharpens your rudder discipline, crosswind technique, and ground handling skills in a way that benefits every type of flying.

Three-point and wheel landing techniques
Crosswind operations and wind correction
Ground loops — recognition and prevention
Solo sign-off upon demonstrated proficiency
↻
Spin Endorsement
CFI Prerequisite · FAR 61.183(i) · Limited Locations

Required for CFI applicants. Spin training develops a deep understanding of aerodynamic stalls, incipient spin entry, developed spin characteristics, and recovery procedures.

Stall series, incipient and developed spin entry
One-turn, multi-turn, and cross-control spin recovery
Emergency spin recovery procedures
Required for CFI applicants under FAR 61.183

KLAL (Lakeland) & KTIX (Titusville)

Flight Review (BFR)
Currency Requirement · FAR 61.56 · Every 24 Months

Every certificated pilot must complete a flight review every 24 calendar months to exercise pilot-in-command privileges. Our CFIs conduct thorough, efficient reviews at all partner locations.

Minimum 1 hr ground + 1 hr flight (varies by pilot)
Covers applicable FARs and safe operating procedures
Logbook endorsement upon satisfactory completion
Available at all 8 partner locations

Instrument
Proficiency

If your instrument currency has lapsed — or you simply want to sharpen your IFR skills — the IPC gets you back on track with a thorough review of instrument procedures, approaches, and airspace operations conducted with one of our qualified CFIIs.

The IPC is not a checkride. It's a proficiency flight conducted to the Instrument ACS standards, covering the areas required by FAR 61.57(d). Upon successful completion, you're restored to full instrument currency.

Pre-flight planning & weather analysis
Instrument takeoff and departure
En-route IFR operations
